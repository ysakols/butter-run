Churn Guide
How to make butter on a run
What You Need
1

Heavy cream (36%+ fat)

About 1 cup (250 ml). Light cream won't work.

2

Two zip-lock bags

Fill 60-70% full. Double-bag for leak safety.

3

Running vest or pack

Tuck bags in. Leave slightly loose to bounce.

🌡️ Temperature + Body Heat

Start cold — straight from the fridge. Your body heat gradually warms it, which actually helps reach the ideal churning temperature (50-59°F).

Cool days (below 65°F): No ice needed. Body heat is your friend. Back pocket placement works best.

Warm days (above 72°F): Add a small ice pack next to the bag. Use an outer mesh pocket for airflow.

Above 68°F cream temp = failure. Fat won't separate. Greasy cream, not butter.

The 5 Stages
💧LiquidStart
🫧Foamy~2 km
☁️Whipped~3 km
🔸Breaking~5 km
🧈Butter!6-10km
Squeeze the bag every 2-3 km. Trails churn faster than roads.
Tips
🧂 Add a pinch of salt before sealing
🧊 Freeze ice cubes with cream on warm days
🏔️ Hills and stairs speed up churning
🥖 Bring bread to eat your butter at the finish
⏱️ Expect 45-60 min of running (6-10 km)
🌡️ Morning runs in cool weather work best
